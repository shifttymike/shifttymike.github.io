Hello, blog

Time to get back into the swing of things

June 17, 2020

Michael

3-Minute Read

Twitter

Facebook

Reddit

LinkedIn

Pinterest

Email

I’ve had a Wordpress blog for about as long as I’ve had a domain.

It was appropriate in 2012, sure, but I like to think that I’m smarter and wiser now, and if I have a Wordpress blog, then I’d be a hypocrite if I made fun of it.

To allow myself some additional liberties, it’s finally (way past) time for WP to die!

“But why”, I hear you cry, “do you need a blog at all? You haven’t updated the other one in years. How is this any different?"

Well, that’s true I suppose. I give a brief overview of it in the “about” page, but there are a few reasons for re-vitalising a personal blog and I’ll go into a bit more detail here:

Blog posts are a good way to “finish” a project

Something I’ve been struggling with lately is finding a project that feels like it’s worth doing, and a reason to stick to it and follow-through. Having a blog in itself doesn’t solve this issue, but it does play an important role.

For example, wanting to figure out how the heck Kubernetes works is a bit of a vague goal, and difficult to know when I’ve achieved that. But writing a blog post about what I’ve learned forces me to understand what I’m explaining.

There’s also occasionally an opinion or some niche knowledge I’d like to share, and it can get lost in the Twitter noise and/or 280 characters don’t explain it well and/or a thread feels out of place. Blog post! Then it’s there and I can point to it as I please.

Blog posts are documentation for others, but also mostly for me

I’ve lost count of the number of times I’ve embarked on and completed a project, or learned a new technology and assumed that I’d remember what I learned. Only to find that, surprise!, I don’t. A friend of mine has a saying, in the context of documenting his work:

Future Albert isn’t as smart as he thinks he is.

This resonated with me, because it’s true. Documentation being purely for the end-user is a misnomer, and results in lacklustre documentation. You might remember the idea as a whole but the detail is often lost - sometimes as a faded memory and sometimes as a magic-sauce command line parameter left unsaved in an ever-growing Sublime Text session.

Write. It. Down.

You will thank yourself later.

Blog posts (and GitHub) are the tech-equivalent of a portfolio

This one is pretty obvious once you’ve read the header, but a blog (and a decent one at that) is probably the best way, along with GitHub, to show the world what you can do and what you’re interested in. You can tell people what you’ve done in a CV, but with a portfolio, you can actually show them.

So here we are! One post in, hopefully quite a few to go over the next couple of months. Thanks for taking the time to read this and feel free to reach out if you have something to say :)
